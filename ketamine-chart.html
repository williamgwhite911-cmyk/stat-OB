Reference notes:
• Ketamine: 500 mg / 10 mL = 50 mg/mL. Dose (mg) ÷ 50 = mL.
• Rocuronium: 100 mg / 10 mL = 10 mg/mL. Dose (mg) ÷ 10 = mL. PHI standing-order RSI dose 1.5 mg/kg IBW IV; alternative 2 mg/kg IBW IM (onset ~5 min).
• Peds weights from PHI Air Medical Handtevy v6.0.0 tier table.
• Adult IBW via Devine formula: M = 50 + 2.3 × (in over 5 ft); F = 45.5 + 2.3 × (in over 5 ft). Sub-5 ft uses negative inches.
• Use IBW for ketamine RSI dosing (lipophilic but standard PHI practice). For obese patients, consider lean body weight rather than actual.
• Caution: 4 mg/kg/hr is the PHI MAX continuous infusion rate. Typical maintenance is 1-2 mg/kg/hr. Start low and titrate. Always BOLUS first (1-2 mg/kg IBW) before initiating drip to avoid under-sedation and dysphoria.
• Rocuronium: Onset 60-90 sec IV, duration 30-60 min. Ensure post-RSI sedation drip is RUNNING before paralytic wears off — paralyzed-but-awake is a critical event. PHI alternative IM dose is 2 mg/kg.
• Agitation 3 mg/kg dose is for IM route per typical excited-delirium / dangerous-agitation practice. IV agitation dose is 1-2 mg/kg.
• PHI: Pediatric ketamine for agitation requires medical control (no peds standing orders per PHI Dangerous Agitation 6.3).
