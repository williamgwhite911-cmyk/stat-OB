⚠ Pregnancy is a difficult airway by default.
Anticipate: airway and oropharyngeal edema · friable nasal mucosa · decreased FRC (rapid desat) · increased aspiration risk (delayed gastric emptying, decreased LES tone) · weight gain alters cricoid landmarks · fewer than 8 weeks of breath-hold reserve.
Mitigations: ramped position with left lateral tilt 15-30°, aggressive pre-O2 (3-4 min NRB at 15 L/min, NC at 15 L/min for apneic O2), smaller ETT (6.0-7.0 cuffed), backup BIAD ready, surgical airway plan briefed.
⚡ OB Max-Dose RSI Strategy (Recommended)
PHI Formulary Note
What PHI carries and orders for RSI:
✓ Ketamine — preferred RSI induction agent (1.5-2 mg/kg IV, max RSI 2 mg/kg). Max infusion 4 mg/kg/hr adult.
✓ Rocuronium — PHI standing order, 1.5 mg/kg IBW IV or 2 mg/kg IM.
△ Succinylcholine — PHI standing order is for Dangerous Agitation only (4 mg/kg IM). Not a PHI-listed routine RSI agent. Avoid in OB whenever roc is available.
✗ Etomidate — NOT on PHI Health formulary. Listed in app for reference only.

Pre-Intubation Optimization
  1. Position: Ramped (ear to sternal notch), left lateral tilt 15-30° to displace gravid uterus and reduce aortocaval compression.
  2. Pre-oxygenate: NRB at 15 L/min for at least 3-4 min, OR BVM with PEEP valve. Add NC at 15 L/min for apneic oxygenation.
  3. Optimize hemodynamics: IV fluids running, push-dose epi or norepi drip prepped if hypotensive (induction will worsen BP).
  4. Aspiration risk: Cricoid pressure is operator-dependent — release if it impairs view.
  5. Backup plan briefed: Plan A (DL/VL), Plan B (BIAD i-gel), Plan C (surgical cric). Verify failed-airway gear is open and accessible.
  6. Mag interaction: Magnesium potentiates BOTH succinylcholine and rocuronium — anticipate prolonged paralysis. Avoid sux if possible in eclamptic on mag.
Post-Intubation Confirmation & Care
  1. EtCO2 sustained ≥ 4 waveforms over 6 breaths — gold standard.
  2. Auscultate epigastrium first, then bilateral chest. Watch for chest rise.
  3. Secure tube — note depth at the lip (typically 21 cm female).
  4. Post-intubation labs/vitals: recheck BP within 2 min (induction commonly causes hypotension). NEW SpO2 target, EtCO2 30-35 mmHg.
  5. Transition to vent — see Vent/Airway tab. Ensure analgesia + sedation drips running before paralytic wears off.
  6. Continue fetal monitoring if pre-delivery. Maternal hypoxia and hypotension are the leading causes of fetal compromise during induction.
