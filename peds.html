⚠ Pediatric airway considerations
Anticipate: large occiput in infants (shoulder roll), narrow subglottis (cricoid is narrowest in <8 yr), short trachea (mainstem easily), high O2 consumption (rapid desat — 2-3× faster than adult), small functional reserve.
Mitigations: shoulder roll for infant/toddler, aggressive pre-O2 with NRB + apneic NC at 5-15 L/min, age-appropriate ETT (cuffed: age/4 + 3.5), rescue BIAD ready, weight-based dosing.
⚡ Peds Max-Dose RSI Strategy (Recommended)
PHI Formulary Note
What PHI carries and orders for peds RSI:
✓ Ketamine — preferred peds RSI agent (1-2 mg/kg IV / 4-5 mg/kg IM). Drip per PHI practice up to 4 mg/kg/hr (PICU literature typically caps routine sedation at 2 mg/kg/hr).
✓ Rocuronium — PHI standing order, 1-1.2 mg/kg IV.
△ Succinylcholine — 1-2 mg/kg IV / 4 mg/kg IM. Caution under age 5 (occult myopathy, hyperkalemic arrest risk).
✗ Etomidate — NOT on PHI Health formulary.
